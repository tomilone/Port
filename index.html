About Me

Hello…

My name is Tony Milone and I’m a Full-Stack Web Developer who is currently based in the Chicagoland Area.

I’m truly passionate about creating things, problem solving, and learning. Through my experiences in sports at a young age and in college, I learned the values of working on a team and growing with others. Becoming a programmer is one of the ways that I’ve been able to put these passions together. I enjoy the constant challenges presented from coding, the process of working & learning with others to tackle those problems, & the satisfaction gained upon solving those problems.

My primary tech stack currently includes Javascript, Node.js, React, Redux, PostgreSQL, HTML5, CSS3.

Outside of coding, some of my hobbies include: drawing, video games, music, coaching baseball, boxing, and spending time with my friends & family.

It Takes A Village

It Takes a Village is a community based altruism application that lets users notify their community that they are in need of assistance, be it food, clothing, transportation, etc. A user can post a story explaining what kind of help they are in need of, and other users in their real world "community" (based off geo-location proximity) can indicate whether they are able to provide aid, or can share to others in their off-app communities that might be able to help.

HTML5, CSS3, Javascript, React, Node.js, PostgreSQL

Github Repository Live App

TryIt

TryIt! is a full stack forum application that encourages users to try something new, or help others find something to do! The user can filter posts by specific tags, which are selected by the user upon creation of a post. If a post catches your eye, expand the post to view more details and let the user know if you tried it!

HTML5, CSS3, Javascript, React, Node.js, PostgreSQL
